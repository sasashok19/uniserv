# UniServe — Digital Marketing Campaign Brief

**Campaign codename: "One Ticket" · 6-week flight · Lean budget (INR) · Ready to execute**

> Companion to `UniServe_IMC_Strategy.md`. This brief inherits that document's positioning statement, messaging house, and consistency rules — do not rewrite messaging here; execute it.

---

## 1. Campaign Objective & Single-Minded Proposition

**Business objective:** Generate the first repeatable pipeline — **30 qualified demo bookings and 6 pilot commitments in 6 weeks** from mid-market private-sector CX teams, at near-zero paid cost, while building the founder's LinkedIn presence as a durable channel.

**Marketing objective:** Make 10,000+ Indian CX/ops decision-makers aware that a working, India-built alternative exists for WhatsApp+email complaint intake — and that its AI completes tickets *before* agents see them.

**Single-minded proposition (SMP):**

> **"Your customer's WhatsApp message becomes a complete, prioritised ticket — before an agent touches it."**

Everything in this campaign demonstrates that one sentence, live, on screen.

---

## 2. Primary Audience — Beachhead Recommendation

**Recommended beachhead: mid-market private-sector CX teams (10–100 agents) in NBFC/lending, auto service networks, and D2C/e-commerce — "Persona 2: Priya" from the IMC strategy.**

**Why this beachhead (and not govt-first):**

1. **Sales cycle:** 1–4 months vs 9–18 for government tenders. The campaign can show conversion inside its own flight window.
2. **Channel fit is 100% live:** these buyers need exactly what works today — WhatsApp inbound + email + AI intake + SLA analytics. No roadmap dependency.
3. **Pain is quantified in their OKRs:** first-response time, duplicate ticket rate, RBI grievance timelines — UniServe's demo maps directly to their metrics.
4. **Budget authority is concentrated:** a Head of CX or COO can approve a pilot without a tender.
5. **Govt lighthouse continues in parallel, BD-led, off-campaign** — one discom/municipal pilot cultivated through warm intros and the architecture whitepaper (see IMC §6.2). It gets *no paid media*; this campaign's assets (demo video, architecture PDF) are reused in those meetings.

**Audience definition for targeting:**

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| Titles | Head of Customer Experience/Service, COO, VP Operations, Head of Contact Centre, Customer Service Head |
| Industries | NBFC/lending, insurance, auto (dealership & service networks), e-commerce/D2C, consumer services |
| Company size | 51–1,000 employees (proxy for 10–100 agents) |
| Geography | India (metro + tier-1 focus: Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, Pune) |
| Watering holes | LinkedIn, CX community WhatsApp groups, BFSI/CX newsletters |

**Secondary audience (organic spillover only):** hospital administrators & university registrars — the anonymity angle (Week 5 content) is seeded for them but gets no paid spend this flight.

## 3. Big Idea + Message Variants

### Big idea: **"The Third Ticket"**

Dramatise the absurdity every CX leader recognises: the same customer complains on WhatsApp, then emails, then calls — and your system files three tickets, each missing half the details. UniServe kills the third ticket (and the second), and delivers the first one *complete*. Every asset opens on the fragmentation problem and resolves on a single, complete, prioritised ticket on the UniServe dashboard.

### Message variants (for A/B rotation)

**Variant A — Pain/duplicate (rational):**
> "Same customer. Same complaint. Three tickets. Your SLA report is fiction and your agents are archaeologists. UniServe stitches WhatsApp and email into one identity, one thread, one ticket."

**Variant B — AI intake (capability):**
> "30% of tickets arrive incomplete — no account number, no location, no order ID. UniServe's AI agent asks the missing questions in-chat, validates the answers, and hands your team a complete, priority-scored ticket. Live today, on WhatsApp and email."

**Variant C — Origin story (emotional/credibility):**
> "We watched a government complaint desk file the same grievance three times and race a 15-minute window on angry tweets. So we built the system that desk needed. It's called UniServe, it's working, and it runs wherever your data must live."

## 4. Channel Plan

### 4.1 LinkedIn — organic (founder-led) — PRIMARY CHANNEL
- **Cadence:** 3 posts/week from founder profile(s); company page reshares +1 native post/week.
- **Content mix per week:** 1 story/insight post (TNEB observations, build-in-public), 1 product-proof post (screen recording ≤60s or annotated screenshot), 1 engagement post (poll, hot take on CX metrics, "what % of your tickets arrive complete?").
- **Rules:** every product post shows the *real* UI; comments answered within 2 hours during business hours; CTA rotates between demo-video link and "DM me 'pilot'".

### 4.2 LinkedIn — paid (lean)
- **Budget:** ₹60,000 total (see §7).
- **Phase 1 (Weeks 2–3):** boost the 2 best-performing organic posts (Thought Leader Ads format) to the §2 audience — ₹20K.
- **Phase 2 (Weeks 4–6):** single-image + video ads running Variants A/B/C against the demo landing page — ₹40K. Optimise to landing-page clicks first, then to demo bookings once ≥20 conversions inform the pixel.

### 4.3 Founder-led content (beyond LinkedIn)
- 1 long-form origin-story essay (Week 1) on the landing-page blog, cross-posted to LinkedIn Articles.
- 2 podcast/community-webinar guest pitches sent Week 1 (Indian SaaS/CX shows); target 1 recorded appearance within flight.

### 4.4 Email outreach sequences (cold + warm)
- **List:** 300 hand-built contacts matching §2 (LinkedIn Sales Nav free trial + public sources); 100 warm network contacts (IIM Calcutta alumni in BFSI/auto/e-comm).
- **Warm sequence (2 touches):** personal note + demo video → follow-up with pilot one-pager.
- **Cold sequence (4 touches over 3 weeks):**
  1. Problem email — "how many of your WhatsApp complaints become duplicate tickets?" (no link, reply-seeking)
  2. Proof email — 60-sec demo GIF/link: WhatsApp → complete ticket
  3. Comparison email — "6 things a grievance-first platform does that a helpdesk doesn't" (capability table)
  4. Break-up email — pilot offer: "2 weeks, your real channels, success criteria agreed upfront"
- **Deliverability hygiene:** dedicated sending subdomain, ≤50 sends/day ramp, plain-text style, honest unsubscribe line.

### 4.7 WhatsApp Business showcase angle
- **"Message us to see it" loop:** a WhatsApp number on the landing page where prospects can *file a test complaint against the demo tenant* and watch UniServe's identity gate and intake questions interrogate them in real time — the product demos itself. Auto-response includes their ticket ID and a link to the public status-lookup page.
- Guardrails: demo tenant only, seeded data, rate-limited, clearly labelled "demo environment"; comply with WhatsApp Business/Meta policy (session messaging, no unsolicited outbound — inbound-initiated only, which is exactly what's live).

## 10. Compliance Notes — Claiming AI Capabilities Honestly

1. **Live vs roadmap is a hard line.** May claim as live: email (IMAP/SMTP, ticket threading), WhatsApp inbound, AI conversation agent with identity gate and configurable intake fields, auto-response/bounce filtering, AI priority scoring with admin-editable rubric, full lifecycle + audit trail, RBAC dashboard + analytics, anonymous complaints (ANON-XXXX) + public status lookup, auto-close, multi-tenancy, cloud/on-prem/hybrid deployability. Must always be labelled **roadmap**: Twitter/X ministerial-tag alerting, IVR, web chat, outbound WhatsApp, SMS, PII scrubbing (Presidio) + at-rest encryption, multi-LLM/air-gapped models.
2. **No autonomy overclaims.** The AI gathers information, classifies, and prioritises; humans resolve tickets. Never say "AI resolves complaints." Approved phrasing: "AI-assisted intake and prioritisation."
3. **Disclose the fallback as a strength**, when asked: a deterministic rule-based flow runs when no LLM is configured — this is a reliability feature, phrase it as such; don't imply LLM behaviour in LLM-off mode.
4. **Third-party dependency honesty:** current default LLM is OpenAI (gpt-4o-mini); if a prospect asks about data flow to LLM providers, answer accurately — and note that hybrid/PII-stripped and local-model modes are roadmap. For sensitive demos, offer the deterministic mode.
5. **Origin-story framing:** describe TNEB as "observed at a state utility's complaint desk" in public assets — a firsthand observation, **not** a customer, deployment, or endorsement. Never use TNEB's name in ads or imply affiliation; the full name may be used in the narrative essay as personal experience.
6. **Demo environment labelling:** the WhatsApp demo line and any public demo use seeded, fictional data and say so on screen.
7. **Email & messaging compliance:** honest sender identity and working unsubscribe on all cold email; WhatsApp interactions inbound-initiated only (Meta policy); no purchased lists claimed as opted-in.
8. **Stat hygiene:** the 3-tickets / 30% / 15-minute figures are firsthand observations from one desk — always framed as such ("observed at…"), never as industry statistics. Market-size numbers only from the IMC doc, always labelled estimates.
